Seeking the familiar...

Typeconnection's advice on seeking the similar states that matching a, g, and e's can imply a good match. Oswald's all look like a narrower carbon-copy of Open Sans'. The biggest noticeable difference is an angling of the line at the top of the lowercase g on Oswald, while it's flat in Open Sans. My other justifications for this pairing include:

  • Oswald and Open Sans were created to enhance legibility, particularly on digital devices; they're 'simplistic' with similar backgrounds.
  • While the two fonts are very similar, the differences work well in complementing one another. Oswald looks narrower and slightly more eye-catching. Using similar fonts, with one acting as headers and titles is a winning strategy according to Typeconnection; Oswald is a clear contender for this.
  • Alternate Gothic, which Oswald is a reworking of, is great for short, legible, yet powerful text. A famous use of it is for the Youtube logo. Meanwhile, Open Sans is great for longform text.
